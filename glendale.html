Welcome to Glendale!

Fun Fact:

Why is this city named Glendale? No one knows. It's origin is an English boy’s name meaning “wooded valley” or “glen”.. yet Glendale when settled and established, was just an empty desert. W.J. Murphy, the builder of the Arizona Canal in 1883-1885, referred to the area as “Glendale” when sold to pay off debts on the canal. He would call the area "their Glendale Castle" in love letters to his wife. But the reason behind choosing this name remains a mystery to this day.

Glendale History

Glendale, Az was founded in 1982 and settled for agriculture. The Arizona canal was a major piece of the city's existance. It was completed in 1886 and William Bartlett started a 640-acre fruit farm known still today as Saguaro Ranch. Glendale Temperance Colony was found in February 27, 1892 and advertisemts were sent throughout the U.S. for settlers. The Luke Air force base began construction in 1941. In 2006 a multi-purpose football stadium opened whish hosts the Arizona Cardinals and Glendale hosted the 2008 and 2015 Superbowls. For a more detailed history of Glendale click here.
